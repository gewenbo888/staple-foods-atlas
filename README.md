# Staple Foods Atlas · 主食图谱

A bilingual, data-driven, single-page comparison of the world's nine major staple foods — Rice, Wheat, Corn, Potatoes, Cassava, Oats, Barley, Sorghum, Sweet Potatoes — across 10 dimensions. Every section, paragraph, table cell, and label is rendered **strictly side-by-side in English and 中文**.

## Sections

1. Overview — definition, agricultural origins, global share
2. Nutrition — calories, protein, carbs, fat, fiber, GI per 100g (full data table)
3. Yield & Efficiency — t/ha, water L/kg, fertilizer, EROI (data table)
4. Environment — CO₂e, land use, water, methane (data table)
5. Cultivation & Geography — per-staple growing region cards
6. Cultural Significance — civilizations and identity dishes
7. Economics — global production, trade %, top producer + **Country Diet Profiles** for 8 countries (CN, IN, US, MX, NG, ET, PE, RU)
8. Processing & Consumption — industrial uses, food chain
9. Health — long-term dietary effects, deficiency diseases
10. Future — climate impact, GMO, lab alternatives, regenerative agriculture
+ **Interactive Best-For Ranker** — click tabs (Health · Environment · Water · Cost · Climate) to see the top 5 staples for each goal, with bilingual reasoning

## Design

- Plain HTML / CSS / JS, no build step
- Strict side-by-side bilingual layout: 2-column grid throughout
- Warm earth-tone palette: dark `#0e0a04` / light `#faf3e0`, amber primary + green/gold/brown supporting
- Cormorant Garamond italic display + Space Grotesk body + Noto Serif/Sans SC for Chinese + JetBrains Mono labels
- Dark/light theme toggle
- Mobile-responsive (stacks bilingual columns below 720px)

## Disclaimer

Nutrition values per 100g raw, sourced from USDA Food Composition Database. Yield, water, fertilizer figures from FAOSTAT global means. Environmental footprints from Poore & Nemecek (2018). Country diet profiles from FAOSTAT food balance sheets (2023). All values approximate; ranges compress active scientific debate.
